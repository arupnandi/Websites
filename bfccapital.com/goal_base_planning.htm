Goal Base Planning
We, at BFC Capital believe that sometimes one should focus more on achieving financial goals rather than the returns expected.

For Example, Mr. “X” requires Rs. 25,00,000/- for the education of his child after ten years and say he is required to invest Rs. 11000/- per month by assuming the expected rate of return @ 12% per annum. Now please appreciate the fact that getting a return of 12% p.a is just an assumption which may go right or may go wrong depending upon the market dynamics but irrespective of anything, one thing is certain and that is Mr. X will be requiring Rs. 25,00,000/- at the end of ten year for an unavoidable cause of child education.

To Achieve this goal what Mr. X should do is to get the periodic review of the percentage of achievement of the goal done to ascertain whether he will able to achieve the goal in particular time duration or not and, if for any reason whatsoever, there is a shortfall, that should be fulfilled by fresh infusions as the priority is to achieve the specified goal.

Financial goals can be achieved by investing periodically or lump sum as per the convenience or availability of the fund. Usually, monthly financial planning is done to achieve various financial goals.

After interacting with a number of our members, we have compiled various financial goals one can achieve with Goal Based planning.
